Lesson On Debugging

Teaching at Cheltenham High School has been an invaluable experience that has allowed me to apply and enhance the skills acquired in my computer science pedagogy class. One fundamental skill I honed through this experience is the concept of backwards design. It involves planning a lesson or project by first identifying the desired learning outcomes and then crafting activities and assessments to meet those objectives. This approach has proven effective in creating well-structured and purposeful lessons.

In addition to curriculum planning, my time at Cheltenham High School has significantly contributed to the development of my public speaking and presentation skills. Conducting lessons and delivering information in a clear and engaging manner was essential to fostering an effective learning environment. This experience has not only bolstered my confidence as an educator but has also honed my skills in articulating concepts to students in a clear manner.

One notable teaching moment involved instructing students on the crucial skill of debugging during a presentation. The session focused on practical problem-solving strategies, emphasizing the importance of identifying and fixing errors in code. Following the presentation, I implemented an interactive approach by having students engage in hands-on debugging activities using code.org code that I had created. This hands-on experience not only reinforced their understanding of debugging techniques but also encouraged collaborative problem-solving within the classroom.
